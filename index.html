I am a Neuroscience PhD student in Prof. Michele Basso's lab at the University of Washington, Seattle. My work focuses on developing experimental and computational frameworks to investigate neural microcircuitry underlying perceptual decision-making in rodents and humans (healthy and clinical) with the help of cutting-edge technologies such as electrophysiology, optogenetics and computational modeling.

I completed my BS and MS in Neuroscience from the Indian Institute of Science Education and Research, India in 2017 and received MA in Psychology from the University of California, Los Angeles in 2021.

I am a DIY enthusiastic and always interested in learning new computational, electrical and surgical skills.
