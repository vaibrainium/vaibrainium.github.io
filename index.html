I am a Neuroscience PhD student in Prof. Michele Basso's lab at University of Washington, Seattle. My work focuses on developing experimental and computational frameworks to investigate neural microcircuitary underlying perceptual decision-making in rodents and humans (healthy and clinical) with the help of cutting-edge technologies such as electrophysiology, optogenetics and computational modelling.

I completed his BS and MS in Neuroscience from Indian Institute of Science Education and Research, India in 2017 and MA in Psychology from University of California, Los Angeles in 2021.

I like DIY projects. I am always interested in learning new skills may it be computational, electronical or surgical.
